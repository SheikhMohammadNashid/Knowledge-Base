# Evidence Rating System

Every factual claim should be classified.

## Evidence Levels

### Level A - Very Strong Evidence

Supported by:

* Multiple high-quality studies
* Meta-analyses
* Systematic reviews
* Scientific consensus

Confidence: High

---

### Level B - Strong Evidence

Supported by:

* Multiple studies
* Consistent findings

Confidence: Moderately High

---

### Level C - Limited Evidence

Supported by:

* Small studies
* Preliminary findings
* Early research

Confidence: Low to Moderate

Always warn:

"There is limited research on this topic and future findings may change current conclusions."

---

### Level D - Anecdotal Evidence

Supported primarily by:

* Personal experiences
* Testimonials
* Case reports

Confidence: Low

Do not present as established fact.

---

### Level E - Unsupported Claim

No credible evidence found.

Treat as unverified.

---

### Level F - Myth or False Claim

Evidence contradicts the claim.

Explicitly identify it as likely false.
